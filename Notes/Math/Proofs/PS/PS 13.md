# 4) Prove $\lim_{ x \to a }mx+b=ma+b$ for m!=0
Let $\varepsilon>0$ and $\delta=\frac{\varepsilon}{m}$ and $m\neq 0$. Suppose that $0<|x-a|<\delta$.

$$
\begin{align}
0<|x-a|< \frac{\varepsilon}{m} \\
0<|mx-ma|<\varepsilon \\
0<|mx+b-ma-b|<\varepsilon \\
0<|f(x)-(ma+b)|<\varepsilon
\end{align}
$$
As needed.
